Project Brief: Emerson & Quinn Website

Overview

Build a simple, modern, responsive website for Emerson & Quinn, a consulting business that helps small, family-run businesses improve operations and stay competitive using AI, automation, and practical systems.

The tone should feel:
 • Approachable, human, and trustworthy
 • Not overly “salesy” or corporate
 • Focused on partnership and shared goals
 • Family-oriented and purpose-driven

⸻

Core Messaging

Primary Value Proposition

Help small businesses:
 • Stay competitive in a rapidly changing world
 • Improve operations and efficiency
 • Increase profitability through practical systems and AI

Key Differentiators
 • Partnership mindset (“from our family to yours”)
 • Focus on real, measurable improvements
 • Performance-based model (only emphasized lightly)
 • Specialization in durable, service-based industries

⸻

Target Audience
 • Owners of small to mid-sized, family-run businesses
 • Industries:
 • Home services (HVAC, plumbing, etc.)
 • Property management
 • Fitness/wellness
 • Contractors/construction

These users:
 • Have limited time
 • Are not highly technical
 • Care about their business and family
 • Want practical, understandable solutions

⸻

Site Structure (Single Page Preferred)

1. Hero Section

Goal: Quickly communicate purpose and build trust
 • Headline:
“Helping Family Businesses Thrive in an Ever-Changing World”
 • Subheadline:
Short paragraph emphasizing partnership, AI + systems, and “from our family to yours”
 • CTA Button:
“Start With a Free Consultation”

⸻

2. How We Work

Goal: Make process simple and easy to understand

3-step layout:
 1. Listen & Learn
 2. Collaborate & Improve
 3. Grow & Measure

Short descriptions (1–2 sentences each)
Keep language simple and jargon-free

⸻

3. Why We Do This

Goal: Communicate purpose (the “why”)

Content themes:
 • Built for the founder’s daughters / future
 • Helping families stay competitive
 • Supporting small, family-run businesses

Tone:
 • Personal, but still professional
 • Avoid overly emotional or long storytelling

⸻

4. Industries Section

Goal: Help users quickly self-identify

List with simple descriptions:
 • Trades & Home Services
 • Property Management
 • Wellness & Fitness
 • Construction & Contractors

⸻

5. Results / Impact

Goal: Show practical outcomes without hype

Bullet-style examples:
 • Increased revenue/profit
 • Better scheduling and operations
 • Improved customer experience
 • Reduced inefficiencies

⸻

6. Call to Action

Goal: Drive conversion without pressure

Text:
“Let’s make your business stronger — together.”

Button:
“Schedule a Free Consultation”

⸻

Design Guidelines

Style
 • Clean, minimal, modern
 • Warm and approachable (not overly corporate)
 • Plenty of whitespace
 • Easy to scan (short sections, clear hierarchy)

Typography
 • Simple, readable sans-serif
 • Strong hierarchy (headline > subheadline > body)

Color Direction
 • Neutral base (white / light background)
 • One primary accent color (trustworthy: blue/green tones)
 • Avoid overly bright or “techy” neon styles

⸻

Functional Requirements
 • Fully responsive (mobile-first)
 • Fast load time
 • Simple navigation (or single-scroll page)
 • CTA buttons scroll to/contact form

⸻

Optional (If Implemented)
 • Contact form:
 • Name
 • Email
 • Business type
 • Short message
 • Basic analytics hook (placeholder ok)

⸻

Tone Rules (Important)
 • Avoid buzzwords like “revolutionary,” “cutting-edge,” etc.
 • Avoid heavy jargon or technical explanations
 • Write like you’re talking to a business owner, not pitching a VC
 • Emphasize partnership, clarity, and trust
